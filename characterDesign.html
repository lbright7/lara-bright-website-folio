    • Character Design

    The first task in the second year of the course was to design a character.

    I had used these applications to create my character design:

    - Pencil and paper
    - Adobe Photoshop
    - Adobe Illustrator

    Here is a detailed overview of my creative process when creating Enigma.

    My character design process begun with brainstorming and mindmapping
    about a space character to create an unconventional thought-provoking game.

    After extensive brainstorming and research amongst other games for inspiration, I then sketched my own character silhouette thumbnails to visualise my ideas.

    Then I chose my favourite five silhouettes and aimed to draw them in more detail.

    Then, once satisfied with my designs, I added greater detail.

    After evaluating my designs, I decided to draw them larger and add the most detail.

    Then I chose my favourite design (the one in the middle) and decided to draw it from the front and back view.

    From this rough sketch, I created an orthographic drawing with graphite pencils on A3 size paper.

    From this manual sketch, I scanned it and digitised the drawing with Adobe Illustrator 2019. I also fixed odd body proportions on this application.

    I then applied colour theory knowledge to decide upon a colour scheme for my character.

    I then decided upon a monochromatic colour scheme with a coloured helmet for my character.

    I also applied my font and textual theory when deciding upon a font for my character's name of 'Enigma'

    After deciding on the ‘OCR A Extended’ font, I then added some personalisation for the Enigma name logo.
